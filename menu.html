Tempura and Imitation Crab Easy Poke Drink suggestion; Korean Corn Tea


Ingredients

    Tempura shrimp
    Short grain rice (1 ½ cup)
    Imitation crab
    Spicy mayo
    Unagi
    Avocado
    Fried onions
    Green onion
    Tempura bits
    Mayo/cream cheese
    Sesame seed oil

Wash your rice until water is clear or almost clear, put into a rice cooker or pot and cook to package directions. Let steam, DO NOT REMOVE LID!!


Set tempura shrimp onto an aluminum foil sheet and bake at 475° F, for 20 min until golden brown.


Either grind imitation crab in a grinder, or chop, or strip. Mix with sesame seed oil, mayo/cream cheese to preference, and seasonings to preference (I recommend salt, pepper, onion powder, garlic powder, smoked paprika, and/or Old Bay).


Add imitation crab to an oven safe bowl and let set in the oven with the tempera for about 5 min to let ingredients bloom.


Chop avocado or mix into paste, to preference. Chop green onions.


Final assembly


Add rice to a bowl (you could add furikake for extra taste).


Set avocado on top of rice


Add imitation crab


Chop and set tempura on top of crab


Dress with fried onion


Dress with spicy mayo and/unagi


Plate and serve.


Tuesday


Spaghetti and Meatballs Drink Suggestion: Honey milk


Ingredients


    Spaghetti noodles
    Ground pork
    Tomatoes, ripe to bursting
    Tomato paste, 1 can
    2 tins of anchovies
    Garlic head
    Red onion
    Can of tomato sauce or diced tomato
    Olive oil
    Red cooking wine, or red wine

Spice blend

    Basil leaves
    Oregano
    Thyme
    Parmesan cheese
    Nutmeg
    Kosher salt and pepper

Assemble spice blend, and divide into thirds.


Add one third of the spice blend to ground pork and cut into squares.


Oil the pan and set to medium high.


Brown the pork, let fond form on the bottom of the pan, don't stir too much!


Remove pork and set aside, let cool and put in the fridge.


Add chopped onion to pan, add garlic after onion has lightly browned.


Add salt and pepper and tomato paste.


Deglaze with red wine after garlic browns.


Add anchovies and another third of spice mixture.


Add tomatoes and begin filling with water until the pan is 2/3rds full.


You could add chicken stock to it, or bullion for added flavor.


Let simmer on medium low for 3 hours, stirring every 15-30 min.


Reduce sauce until ⅓ full.


After 3 hours use a hand blender to blend sauce. Add pork back in after. Let simmer for another 2 hrs.


Boil spaghetti noodles to al dente, reserve 4 cups of salted pasta water.


Add the final third of seasoning to sauce and add noodles in, adding pasta water to preference.


Let simmer on lowest for 5 minutes once incorporated.


Plate and serve.

Chilaquiles and Chili Drink Suggestion: Horchata


Ingredients


    Tomatoes
    Chili peppers (guajillo, àrbol, and any preference)
    Onion or shallot
    Garlic
    Corn tortillas rounds
    Oaxaca cheese or feta or anything on hand
    Any choice of protein (Chicken, beef, eggs, tofu, etc.)
    Cilantro leaves

Cut corn tortillas into rounds or squares or any vague edible shape you prefer. Either fry on stove top in oil until golden brown, or bake in an oven at 350° F for 20-30 minutes until golden brown.


(You could add cumin powder, salt, pepper, a bit of lime, maybe some chili powder, and coriander powder if you wanted some extra pizazz. Measure spices with your heart.)


Gently score tomatoes on bottom and boil them for a few minutes until skin starts to peel, immediately shock into ice bath until chilled. Remove tomato skin and dice.


In a pot, boil chilies until chilies are softened, do not reserve water.


Cut onion and grill on stove until one side is lightly charred.


Add softened chilies, charred onions, tomatoes, garlic, and seasonings of choice (recommended; cumin, coriander, salt, pepper, a bay leaf, and a pinch or two of sugar). Add water, and you could add bullion (any kind) for additional flavor.


Blend until smooth and pour over the strainer into a bowl.


Add protein of choice and lightly brown in an oiled pan on medium high heat, add chile and get to a simmer.


Once bubbling, add tortilla chips and cook lightly, maybe 2 minutes. Add cheese at the very end.


Plate and serve.


Thursday


Spicy Peanut Noodles Drink Suggestion; Thai Tea


Ingredients


    Rice noodles (vermicelli preferred)
    Peanut Butter
    Hoisin sauce
    Chili oil
    Garlic powder
    Onion powder
    Brown sugar
    Chinese five spice
    Lime juice

Take your rice noodles and put them into a heat proof bowl.


Boil one tea kettles worth of water, and pour onto rice noodles, let sit for 10 minutes.


After 10 minutes drain and rinse under cold water to stop the cooking process.


Add about 3 scoops of peanut butter to a bowl, add garlic powder, onion powder, five spice, chili oil, brown sugar, hoisin, and lime juice to a heat proof bowl.


Add ½ a cup of boiling water to peanut butter mixture at a time, if you add it too quickly you WILL RUIN THE SAUCE, GO SLOWLY AND BE PATIENT!!


When sauce is at desired thickness add noodles and a little bit more water and mix well.


Optional, add cilantro and shredded carrots, or preferred protein (shrimp and chicken recommend)


Plate and serve.



Friday


America's Favorite, The Hamburger Heart Attack Special Drink Suggestion; Unsweetened Tea with Lemon


Ingredients


    ½ lb or ¼ lb of hamburger, ground beef
    Worcestershire sauce ¼ cup
    Garlic powder
    Onion powder
    Salt
    Pepper
    Cayenne pepper
    Paprika (smoked is recommended but plain is fine too)
    Rosemary and Thyme is Optional but recommended
    Soy Sauce ¼ cup
    A pinch of brown sugar (To Taste)
    A dash of red wine ¼ cup
    Lettuce, tomato, red onion, fried onions, hamburger buns
    Any additional toppings (bacon, blue cheese, american cheese, etc)

Add garlic powder, onion powder, salt, pepper, paprika, brown sugar, rosemary/thyme, cayenne, soy sauce, worcestershire, and red wine into a bowl until fully incorporated.


Add beef and massage with hands until the spice mixture is fully absorbed, let rest in the fridge for 15 minutes when complete.


Prep topping while the hamburger rests, AND WASH YOUR VEGETABLES


Divide hamburger meat into appropriate size, use hands to gently shape them into pancake shapes, let rest in shape for 5 minutes.


Bring the pan to medium high heat, add some canola or vegetable oil to the pan (Not too much, half a cap full is what I recommend) and wait until oil is shimmering.


CAREFULLY add the hamburger to the pan and let sit, DO NOT TOUCH IT, LET IT COOK.


When you see the hamburger change color around the sides, flip over, DO NOT PRESS IT.


Optional; add red onion to the pan to cook in the oil.


Your hands will tell you the doneness of the patty; touch your pointer finger to your thumb and touch the fat part of your thumb, that is Rare.

Your middle finger to thumb is Medium-Rare, ring finger to thumb is Well Done, and pinky to thumb is Very Well Done.

Only touch the patty WITH CLEAN HANDS.


Optionally; add hamburger buns to oil after patties are done to soak the oil up and lightly toast.



Secret Sauce:

    Mayo/Sour Cream, Or 50/50 Mix
    Pickle Juice (Any kind, pickled jalapenos add kick)
    Paprika
    Sriracha Sauce
    Pinch of Sugar
    Garlic Powder
    Onion Powder
    Cumin (1 teaspoon)
    Optional: coriander or cilantro

Mix in a bowl and add to buns before adding lettuce, tomato, etc.


Plate and serve.
